// A bit about me

Technology has been a defining interest throughout my life and the foundation of my career. It started with an early desktop computer and a curiosity about how systems worked behind the scenes; a curiosity that quickly grew into a deeper interest in software, hardware, and the ways technology shapes the world around us. By middle school, I had already decided this was the path I wanted to pursue.

One experience that stands out early on is disassembling and repairing an overheating laptop with no prior repair experience. What began as a simple desire to fix a problem became an opportunity to research, troubleshoot, and learn through direct experience. That hands-on approach has remained central to how I develop technical skills and tackle challenges today.

Outside of work, I've continued expanding my knowledge through personal projects and independent study. I work regularly with Linux-based systems, have built and maintained a small-scale NAS environment, implemented cloud file storage, and managed containerized applications through Docker and Portainer. These projects keep me close to the fundamentals; system architecture, troubleshooting, automation, and secure technology practices.

Professionally, I've had the opportunity to support a diverse range of clients across telecommunications, financial services, and cloud technology. Working across industries has exposed me to complex enterprise environments and reinforced something I believe strongly: adaptability and a security-first mindset matter as much as technical depth.

What draws me to cybersecurity specifically is the challenge of understanding how systems operate, finding where they're vulnerable, and building solutions that make them more resilient. It's a field that rewards curiosity and punishes complacency; which suits me well.

I'm also genuinely interested in the role AI is starting to play across the industry. In my previous role I contributed to AI literacy initiatives, helping teams understand emerging capabilities while thinking carefully about governance and responsible adoption. I think the security implications of AI are one of the more important problems the field will face in the coming years.
